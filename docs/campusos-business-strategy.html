About This Document

This document defines the complete business strategy for CampusOS. It pairs with the frozen technical documents (ERD v10, Architecture Review, Function Library, Dev & Deployment Plan) as the business counterpart — the architecture tells you what to build, this tells you how to sell, support, and grow it.

All key decisions documented in Section 2 have been discussed, debated, and locked. The pricing model uses flat monthly fees by size band with all features included. The community exchange supports 6 listing types with a 20/10/70 three-way revenue split for content and 5% platform fee for surplus assets. The team structure follows a lean/bootstrap approach leveraging the AI development pipeline. The CTO role is titled Chief Information Officer (CIO). Parents can browse, buy, and participate in forums but cannot create listings.

14 core business sections plus a 15th section summarising the completed architecture amendments (v10.1 and v10.2). Platform total: 840 tables, 38 modules, 76 ADRs.

1. Executive Summary

CampusOS is a cloud-native, multi-tenant School Operating System designed to unify every operational domain of a K–12 school into a single platform. The architecture is complete: 803 tables across 36 modules, governed by 70 Architecture Decision Records, with a clear 5-wave delivery plan.

The core thesis: schools currently run on 8–15 disconnected software systems. Each has its own login, data silo, and contract. CampusOS replaces all of them with one platform sharing a single identity model, permission system, and source of truth.

The development pipeline is AI-driven: Claude writes all application code, ChatGPT reviews it, and a human accepts it. This allows a team of 4–5 people to build what would traditionally require 35–45. The lean/bootstrap approach is the chosen path — the AI pipeline makes this viable for a platform of this scope.

3. Pricing Model

3.1 Pricing Philosophy

  • All features included. Every school gets every module. No feature gating. The platform is an operating system — you don’t buy it in pieces.
  • Flat fee by size band. Not per-student. A 50-student Montessori has the same operational complexity as a 150-student school. Per-student ignores non-student value (fleet, facilities, IT, events).
  • Monthly billing, annual discount. 15% off for annual prepayment. Monthly lowers the barrier; discount incentivises commitment and improves cash flow.

4. Founding Team & Hiring Sequence

AI Leverage Model

CampusOS uses a two-AI, one-human development pipeline. Claude writes all code. ChatGPT reviews. A human (the CIO) accepts. This inverts the hiring curve: engineering stays lean while customer-facing roles grow faster. At Month 18, ~11 people build what traditionally requires 35–45. The ~4x leverage applies to engineering; customer-facing roles remain similar.

Timing Is Everything

Nov–Feb: Seed and nurture. Conference circuit. • Mar–May: Close deals — schools building next year’s budget. • Jun–Jul: Implementation window — schools empty, staff available. • Aug: Target go-live. Hypercare. • Sep–Oct: Dead zone for sales. Nurture and collect case studies.

  • Channel 5 — Strategic partnerships: NAIS, ISC, PEPPM, TIPS/TAPS, CMOs (one deal = 5–50 schools), IT consultancies as resellers.
  • Channel 6 — Open marketplace as funnel ($0 marginal): Teacher discovers content via Google, previews but can’t import without account. Bottom-up complements top-down.

Deployment Freeze

No code deployments for the first 3 weeks of the academic year. All summer updates ship by late July. Platform enters freeze until mid-September. Back-to-school is peak load — attendance, scheduling, enrolment, payments all at maximum volume.

Onboarding Capacity

One implementation person can onboard 3–4 schools concurrently. Year 1 capacity (1 lead): 6–8 schools. Year 2 (1 lead + 2 CSMs): 15–20. Year 3+ (team of 4–5): 30–40 new schools/year. Self-service wizard for Micro/Small bands targeted by Year 3.

9. Community Exchange

The CampusOS Community Exchange is the long-term moat. It goes far beyond a teacher content store — it is a unified school community where all users (teachers, students, staff, admins, and parents) share content, trade surplus assets, troubleshoot together, and build public profiles.

9.2 Community Features

  • Universal profiles: Every community participant gets a public profile showing school, role, and for students, grade. Privacy controls let users choose what to display.
  • 5-star ratings and reviews: On all listing types. Verified-purchase ratings weighted higher.
  • Threaded comments: On all listings and forum threads. Moderated. Helpful voting.
  • Top contributors: Reputation scoring from listings, ratings, helpful comments, forum activity. Leaderboards by school, subject, region.
  • Watch lists: Schools post what they need. Auto-notification when matching surplus appears.
  • Unified search: Single search bar returns results from all listing types, forums, and knowledge posts. Weighted by relevance, rating, recency.
  • Surplus asset visibility: Per-listing choice: CampusOS-only, open browse, or fully open. Schools control who sees their listings.

9.3 Revenue Model

Revenue Splits

Content (educational + student portfolio): CampusOS 20% · School 10% · Creator 70%. Students earn the same split as teachers, with parental consent.

Surplus assets & books: CampusOS 5% · Selling school 95%. Parents can buy as individual purchasers.

Field trips & knowledge posts: Free only. No revenue split. Community value and engagement.

9.4 Parent Access

Parents can browse all marketplace listings, buy educational content and surplus assets, participate in parent-filtered forums, comment, rate, and vote. Parents cannot create listings. Asset purchases by parents are personal transactions, not through school accounts. Parent forum categories include back-to-school prep, homework support, recommended reading, and school event coordination.

9.5 Quality Enforcement

  • Gate 1 — Verified source: Educational content must have cls_submissions. Portfolio items must link to pfl_portfolio_items. Assets must link to source inventory. Verified automatically.
  • Gate 2 — Role-appropriate reviewer: Teachers: dept head. Students: assigning teacher. Staff: department head. Librarians: head librarian. Via M2 Approval Workflows.
  • Gate 3 — Building admin/business manager: IP review, brand alignment, pricing. Final gate before listing goes live.

9.6 Launch Sequence

  • Wave 2: Athletic network (opportunistic). Cross-school forums (including parent forums). Universal profiles. Reputation system.
  • Wave 3: Full marketplace with all 6 listing types. Surplus asset exchange. Watch lists. Unified search. Parent purchasing.
  • Wave 3+: Benchmarking (50+ schools). Professional Development. Knowledge base maturation.

15. Architecture Changes — Completed

The business strategy decisions in this document required additions to the CampusOS architecture. These have been designed and documented in two amendments. All changes are platform-schema only — the frozen ERD v10 (803 tables, 36 modules) is unmodified.

15.1 Amendment v10.1 — CRM, Internal Ops, Pricing

17 new platform-schema tables. 4 new ADRs (071–074). Pre-Wave 1 (required before first customer).

  • M90 Customer & Subscriptions (9 tables, crm_): Accounts, subscriptions, invoices, contacts, interactions, onboarding progress, health scores, renewal pipeline. ADR-071.
  • M91 Internal Operations (5 tables, ops_): CampusOS employees, permissions, account assignments, internal tickets, tenant access grants with 4-hour auto-expiry and full audit trail for FERPA/GDPR. ADR-072.
  • Pricing Infrastructure (3 tables): Pricing bands, pricing history, support tier definitions. Links to crm_subscriptions.

See ERD v11 (Platform Business Schema) for full table definitions.

15.2 Amendment v10.2 — Community Exchange

10 new tables, 4 modified. 2 new ADRs (075–076). Supersedes v10.1 Sections 5–6.

  • Polymorphic listings: 6 listing types (educational, student portfolio, field trips, surplus assets, books, knowledge). Per-listing visibility control for assets.
  • Universal profiles: All user types including parents. Privacy controls. Reputation scoring.
  • Comments, ratings, helpful votes: Threaded comments on all content. 5-star ratings. Helpful voting for forum/knowledge ranking.
  • Reputation and leaderboards: Points system. Top contributors by school, subject, region.
  • Watch lists: Schools post needs. Auto-notification on matching surplus listings.
  • Asset exchange: School-to-school and parent-to-school transactions. Condition reports. Dispute resolution.
  • Unified search: PostgreSQL tsvector index across all content types. ADR-076.
  • Parent access: Browse, buy, comment, rate, forums. Cannot create listings.

See ERD v11 (Community Exchange) for full table definitions.
